# MOAT LOGIC & DEFINITIONS

## 1. Moat Size
* **Wide (10+ yrs structural advantage):**
    * *Network Effect:* New users add value to all users; undisputed market leadership.
    * *Switching Costs:* Mission-critical deployment; extremely high financial or operational friction to leave.
    * *Intangible Assets:* Strong brand with absolute pricing power; exclusive patents/licenses.
    * *Low-Cost Production:* Structural, lowest-cost architecture that peers mathematically cannot match.
    * *Counter-Positioning:* Incumbents cannot copy the model without destroying their own core business.
* **Narrow (3-10 yrs structural advantage):**
    * *Network Effect:* Niche network; users are loyal but not totally locked in.
    * *Switching Costs:* Friction is based on habit, convenience, or moderate setup costs.
    * *Intangible Assets:* Brand loyalty exists, but customers remain somewhat price-sensitive.
    * *Low-Cost Production:* Regional or limited scale cost advantages.
    * *Counter-Positioning:* Challenges incumbents, but incumbents have the resources to eventually fight back.
* **None (0 yrs):**
    * Commodity product, zero pricing power, easily replicable, zero switching costs. Competing purely on price with no structural advantage.

## 2. Moat Direction
* **Widening:** Rising engagement, margin expansion, extending brand/utility into adjacent markets.
* **Stable:** Flat growth, stable margins, high retention, but no newly generated advantages.
* **Narrowing:** Losing market share, margin compression, increasing commoditization.
