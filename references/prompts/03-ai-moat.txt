## LOGIC GATES & DEFINITIONS

**1. Liability ("Cost of Failure")**
- **Antifragile:** High stakes where hallucination is catastrophic (e.g., Medical, Cyber, Aerospace).
- **Robust:** Medium stakes—errors are costly but not life-critical; regulated or quality-sensitive domains.
- **Fragile:** Low stakes where 90% accuracy is fine (e.g., Marketing copy, Basic code).

**2. Business Model ("Work vs. Worker")**
- **Antifragile:** Usage/Outcome-based where AI increases transaction volume and scales revenue.
- **Robust:** Hybrid/Transitioning—e.g. historically seat-based but successfully selling "Agent" or compute-based offerings; revenue mix shifting toward usage/outcomes. Acknowledges seat risk but credits agent transition.
- **Fragile:** Seat-based SaaS where AI makes 1 worker do the work of 10, losing 9 seats of revenue.

**3. Physical World ("Atoms vs. Bits")**
- **Antifragile:** Hardware integration (Logistics, robotics, sensors) because AI cannot simulate physical delivery.
- **Robust:** Hybrid—some hardware/operational integration or physical touchpoints; not pure bits but not fully atoms-dependent.
- **Fragile:** Pure software with zero marginal cost that is easily replicated or simulated.

**4. Network ("Data Context" / System of Record)**
- **Antifragile:** Proprietary System of Record with deep data gravity. Private, messy, historical records that horizontal AI cannot replicate; siloed context is the ultimate defense against horizontal AI startups.
- **Robust:** Mixed—some proprietary or vertical data but not yet a system of record; or transitioning from public/horizontal to owned context.
- **Fragile:** Public/Horizontal knowledge trained on the open internet (e.g., StackOverflow, Wikipedia); no proprietary data gravity.
