ORDER OF OPERATIONS (THE RULES)

STEP 1: Base Score (use injected value; do not recalculate)
When BASE_SCORE is injected at the top of this prompt (computed by the pipeline from the 7 framework scores and weights), use that number. Do not perform weighted math yourself—LLMs are error-prone at multi-variable weighted sums. Apply all adjustments and vetoes (Steps 2–5) to the injected BASE_SCORE. If BASE_SCORE is not injected, infer tier from framework evidence and state in WEIGHTED SCORECARD that score was not precomputed. List each framework and its contribution in WEIGHTED SCORECARD for transparency; the arithmetic is done by the script.

Mapping:
- 85-100 = STRONG BUY
- 70-84 = BUY
- 50-69 = HOLD
- 30-49 = SELL
- 0-29 = STRONG SELL
Always use the floor (round down) before mapping the score to a tier.

STEP 2: Phase 5 Value/Dividend Upgrade
The Rule: If Phase is 5 AND 05-sentiment is "Cheap" AND dividend yield is at least 5-6%, upgrade a base-tier HOLD to BUY. State this explicitly in ADJUSTMENTS.

STEP 3: Apply Mandatory Penalties (Narrative & SaaS)
Narrative Flip:
- If a 180-degree thesis reversal is detected, downgrade conviction by exactly one tier.

ROIC Screener Rule (by phase):
- Phase 1 & 2 (Disruptors): Mute ROIC completely. Do not apply any ROIC/ROA veto or cap. Score on revenue growth and margin inflection only.
- Phase 3 (Inflection): If FCF is positive AND margin_inflection is true, ignore trailing ROIC (do not apply capital-efficiency veto). If Phase 3 and (FCF is negative OR margin_inflection is false), apply the Phase 4/5 ROIC/ROA bar (same as mature)—no ambiguity: only when both FCF positive and margin_inflection true do you ignore ROIC.
- Phase 4 & 5 (Mature): Apply the ROIC Hard Veto when ROIC is present: require ROIC > 8% for STRONG BUY. ROIC 4–8% cap at BUY only if valuation is Cheap/Discounted (price ≥15% below primary anchor); otherwise cap HOLD—do not pay a premium for mediocre capital efficiency. ROIC ≤ 4% cap at HOLD. If ROIC is missing (e.g. API did not return balance sheet), do NOT cap at HOLD for missing—fall back to ROA: STRONG BUY ≥ 6%, cap BUY 3–6%, cap HOLD < 3%. Do not let a blank API field ruin the analysis.

Capital Efficiency Guardrail (implementation):
- Treat roic_pct as missing (use ROA fallback) only when it is N/A, null, or empty—i.e. when roic_pct is absent from GUARDRAIL DATA. Do not treat the literal value 0 as missing; if roic_pct is present and 0, apply the ROIC rule (cap HOLD). This avoids the zero-vs-null trap where a parsing error outputs 0 and bypasses the ROA fallback.
- When roic_pct is provided in GUARDRAIL DATA (or ROIC is clearly stated in framework outputs), use ROIC for Phase 4/5 per the Hard Veto above. ROIC 4–8%: BUY only if valuation is Cheap/Discounted; otherwise cap HOLD. ROIC ≤ 4%: cap HOLD. ROIC is the preferred capital-efficiency metric (avoids goodwill distortion vs ROA).
- When ROIC is missing (N/A, null, or empty), use `roa_pct` as the fallback. For Phase 4 or Phase 5 only: STRONG BUY requires ROA ≥ 6%; ROA 3–6% cap at BUY; ROA < 3% cap at HOLD. Do not treat missing ROIC as a veto—always fall back to ROA when ROIC is absent. Do not "metric shop" to justify a buy.
- If the applicable metric (ROIC or ROA) < 1% AND the company is mature, acquisition-heavy, or heavily dependent on capital return optics, treat as a major structural warning and downgrade by one tier unless already capped more severely.
- Do NOT use this guardrail for Phase 1–2 (mute) or for Phase 3 only when both FCF positive and margin_inflection true (ignore ROIC). For Phase 3 when FCF is negative OR margin_inflection is false, apply the Phase 4/5 bar.

STEP 4: Apply the "Valuation Circuit Breaker" (Momentum-Adjusted)
Anchors:
- Use the more conservative primary anchor from `internal_guidance_target` and `analyst_high_target` (the lower of the two when both exist).
- Treat `analyst_mean_target` as lagging context only.
- If no primary anchor is available, treat valuation as Fair for this step and report missing anchors clearly in `VALUATION ANCHORS`.
- If no primary anchor is available, STRONG BUY is NOT allowed—except for Phase 1 or 2 (disruptors), where STRONG BUY is permitted when anchors are missing (early-stage companies often have no internal guidance or reliable analyst targets).

Constraint:
- A STRONG BUY is only allowed if valuation is Fair or Cheap on the conservative primary anchor.

Breaker Rules:
- If price is <=10% above the conservative primary anchor: treat valuation as Fair. No downgrade.
- If price is >10% and <=25% above the conservative primary anchor: valuation is stretched but acceptable. No automatic downgrade. Note caution in ADJUSTMENTS.
- If price is >25% above the conservative primary anchor:
  - If margins are expanding and no narrative flip is detected, do NOT apply an automatic downgrade. Keep the tier and note caution in ADJUSTMENTS.
  - Otherwise, downgrade by one tier and explain the reason in ADJUSTMENTS.

Expensive + Decelerating Growth Cap:
- If 05-sentiment classifies valuation as Expensive AND 02-metrics shows decelerating growth, the final verdict must be capped at HOLD.

Sentiment Gatekeeper (05-Sentiment as Filter—three circuit breakers; apply after base score and valuation breaker):
1. Bull Trap Breaker: If 05-sentiment valuation is Expensive AND (Investors are Bearish OR Media is Negative), cap the final verdict at HOLD. Rationale: You are "fighting the tape"; do not buy a high-quality name in a "falling knife" phase. Exception—Fundamental Dominance: If 02-metrics shows >20% YoY revenue growth AND 07-business indicates High Resilience, do NOT apply the Bull Trap cap; ignore the "Media is Negative" signal so you are not shaken out of a strong turnaround by noisy headlines.
2. Contrarian Upgrade: If the base score is >75 (high quality) AND 05-sentiment valuation is Cheap AND Media is Negative, upgrade a BUY to STRONG BUY. Rationale: "Be greedy when others are fearful"—high-quality business temporarily punished by headline or sector sell-off.
3. Euphoria Penalty: If 05-sentiment valuation is Expensive AND (Analysts or Investors) show High confidence, downgrade a STRONG BUY to BUY. Rationale: DCA rather than "Deploy Capital Immediately"; avoid entering at a local blow-off top.

Institutional Override:
- Treat institutional ownership as supportive only if institutional ownership is at least 70%, or if 05-sentiment explicitly states institutional accumulation/support.
- If institutional ownership is supportive, do NOT downgrade based solely on a premium to a lagging mean target.
- Do NOT claim institutional ownership is increasing unless a trend is explicitly provided in the input.

STEP 4b: Phase 5 Maturity Cap (Avoid Score Inflation on Stable Value Names)
- For Phase 5 (Capital-Return) companies only: STRONG BUY is permitted only if (i) the phase/growth frameworks indicate revenue growth ≥12%, OR (ii) capital efficiency meets the bar (ROIC ≥ 8% when ROIC is supplied, else ROA ≥ 6%) and valuation is Cheap. Otherwise cap the maximum verdict at BUY even if the weighted score is 85+.
- Paths (i) and (ii) are independent: if (i) is satisfied (growth ≥12%), STRONG BUY does not require the 15% discount; valuation must still be Fair or better per Step 4. The 15% "Cheap" definition applies only to path (ii).
- Definition of "Cheap" for (ii) only: Strictly, current price must be at least 15% below the conservative primary anchor (i.e. discount ≥ 15%). A 2% or 5% discount is NOT Cheap; do not treat small discounts as Cheap.
- Rationale: A Phase 5 company with single-digit or low-double-digit growth (e.g. 9–11%) is a "stable value" or "capital return" story, not an "elite growth" story. Reserve STRONG BUY ("deploy capital immediately") for Phase 5 names that show either strong growth or strong capital efficiency plus genuinely cheap valuation; otherwise output BUY ("dollar-cost average") to avoid over-conviction on dead-money risk.

STEP 5: Apply the "Structural Veto" (Final Override)
The Rule:
- If 03-ai-moat is "Fragile" AND 04-strategic-moat is "Narrowing", "Shrinking", or "Eroding", the MAXIMUM allowable rating is HOLD.
- If the math already dictates SELL under this condition, move to STRONG SELL.
